# YouTube Channel Setup Guide

## Story 1: YouTube Channel Setup

**Goal:** Create a professional YouTube channel that clearly communicates veterinary education value and discloses AI assistance.

**Time estimate:** 30-60 minutes

---

## Step 1: Channel Name Ideas

Your channel name should be:
- **Memorable** - Easy to remember and spell
- **Descriptive** - Indicates veterinary/pet health content
- **SEO-friendly** - Includes searchable keywords
- **Available** - Check YouTube and social media

### Option A: Direct/Educational Style

1. **VetTerms Explained** - Clear, direct, SEO-friendly
2. **Pet Health Decoded** - Implies translation of complex info
3. **The Pet Med Translator** - Exactly what it does
4. **Vet Visit Explained** - Covers broader topics beyond just meds
5. **Pet Care Plain English** - Emphasizes simplification

### Option B: Friendly/Approachable Style

1. **Your Pet's Health Guide** - Personal, helpful
2. **Ask About Pet Health** - Invites questions
3. **Pet Health Made Simple** - Clear value proposition
4. **The Pet Parent Playbook** - Broader appeal
5. **Pawsitive Pet Health** - Playful but still professional

### Option C: Authority/Expert Style

1. **Veterinary Insights** - More formal, authoritative
2. **Pet Health Academy** - Educational positioning
3. **The Vet Explainer** - Direct, expert-focused
4. **Pet Medical Guide** - Straightforward
5. **Animal Health Explained** - Broader than just dogs/cats

### My Recommendations (in order):

1. **VetTerms Explained** - Best SEO, clear purpose, memorable
2. **Pet Health Decoded** - Modern, implies you're solving a puzzle
3. **The Pet Med Translator** - Most accurate to what you do

**Action:** Choose a name and check availability:
- YouTube search: youtube.com/@[channelname]
- Instagram/Twitter: In case you expand later

## Step 2: Channel Setup

### Create Your Channel

1. Go to youtube.com and sign in with Google account
2. Click your profile icon ‚Üí "Create a channel"
3. Choose "Use a custom name" (don't use personal name)
4. Enter your chosen channel name
5. Agree to terms and create

### Channel Customization

Go to YouTube Studio ‚Üí Customization

**Basic Info:**
- **Name:** [Your chosen name]
- **Handle:** @[yourname] (this is your unique URL)
- **Description:** See "About Section Copy" below

**Branding:**
- **Profile Picture:** Use Canva to create simple logo (coming up)
- **Banner Image:** 2560 x 1440 pixels (Canva has YouTube banner template)
- **Video Watermark:** Optional - small logo that appears on all videos

## Step 4: Channel Branding (Canva)

### Profile Picture (Logo)

**Option 1: Text-based logo**
1. Open Canva.com (free account)
2. Search "Logo" templates
3. Choose simple, clean design
4. Text: Your channel name or initials
5. Icon: Stethoscope, pet silhouette, medical cross, or paw print
6. Colors: Professional but friendly (blue + white, green + white, or teal + orange)
7. Download as PNG, 800 x 800 pixels

**Option 2: Icon-only logo**
- Simple icon: Stethoscope around a paw print
- Veterinary cross with pet silhouette
- Book with paw print (education + pets)

**Keep it simple** - Logo needs to be readable at small sizes (phone screens)

### Banner Image

1. In Canva, search "YouTube Channel Art"
2. Template is already correct size (2560 x 1440)
3. Include:
   - Channel name (large, readable)
   - Tagline: "Veterinary Information in Plain Language" or "Pet Health Explained Simply"
   - Visual: Pet images (use free stock from Canva library)
   - Optional: Upload schedule "New Videos Mon/Wed/Fri"
4. Keep text in "safe zone" (center area that shows on all devices)
5. Download and upload to YouTube Studio ‚Üí Customization ‚Üí Branding

### Color Scheme Recommendations

**Professional + Friendly:**
- Primary: Blue (#4A90E2) - Trust, medical
- Secondary: Orange (#F5A623) - Friendly, energetic
- Neutral: White/Light Gray - Clean

**Natural + Calm:**
- Primary: Green (#27AE60) - Health, natural
- Secondary: Warm beige (#E8DCC4) - Calm, approachable
- Neutral: White - Clean

**Modern + Trustworthy:**
- Primary: Teal (#16A085) - Balance of friendly + professional
- Secondary: Coral (#FF6B6B) - Warm, approachable
- Neutral: Dark gray text on white background

## Step 8: Growth Settings (Set Now, Pay Off Later)

### Playlists

Create these playlists NOW (even if empty):
1. **"Medications & Prescriptions"** - All videos about meds
2. **"Understanding Vet Terms"** - Terminology explanations
3. **"Common Pet Health Questions"** - General Q&A style videos
4. **"Breed-Specific Health"** - For breed-focused content
5. **"What to Expect"** - Procedures, vet visits, surgeries

**Why:** Playlists encourage binge-watching (more watch time = better algorithm ranking)

### Community Tab

- Not available until you have 500 subscribers
- But when you unlock it, use it for: polls, questions, behind-the-scenes

### End Screen Template

YouTube Studio ‚Üí Content ‚Üí "End screen" (applies to all videos):
- **Subscribe button** (encourages growth)
- **Best for viewer** (algorithm suggests next video)
- **Playlist** (encourage binge watching)

Set this up once, applies to all future videos automatically.

## Legal & Ethical Compliance

### Must-Haves (Non-Negotiable):

**1. AI Disclosure:**
- Channel description states AI-assisted content
- Video descriptions mention AI usage
- You're being transparent, not hiding it

**2. Medical Disclaimer:**
- Every video states "not veterinary advice"
- Channel about section includes disclaimer
- Video descriptions include disclaimer
- Emphasize "consult your vet" in every video

**3. Source Attribution:**
- Every video description lists sources with URLs
- Cite authoritative institutions (not random blogs)
- On-screen citations if specific stat/claim

**4. Avoid:**
- Making diagnosis statements ("Your dog has X")
- Recommending specific treatments ("Give your dog Y")
- Contradicting vet advice ("Your vet is wrong about Z")
- Using fear tactics to get views ("This kills dogs instantly!")
