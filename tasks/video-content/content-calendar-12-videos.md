# Content Calendar: First 12 Videos (Month 1)

## Purpose
Plan 12 video topics for first month (3-4 videos/week) to validate veterinary education channel concept. Topics selected to cover diverse sub-categories and test audience preferences.

**Publishing schedule:** 3 videos/week (Mon/Wed/Fri or Tue/Thu/Sat)

---

## Video Topic Selection Strategy

### Criteria for topics:
1. **Pet owner pain point** - Answers a real question pet owners have
2. **Search volume** - People actually search for this
3. **Authoritative sources available** - Can find Cornell, AVMA, etc. sources
4. **Visual potential** - Can be illustrated with B-roll
5. **Avoids diagnosis/advice** - Educational translation only

### Topic diversity mix:
- **Medications** (3 videos) - High interest, clear value
- **Understanding vet communications** (3 videos) - Test results, estimates, terminology
- **Procedures/visits** (2 videos) - What to expect
- **Cost/financial** (2 videos) - Why things cost what they do
- **Preventive care** (2 videos) - Vaccines, dental

## Video 1: How to Read Pet Medication Labels ⭐ START HERE

**Target length:** 6-7 minutes
**Keyword:** "pet medication label," "understanding prescription labels"
**Search intent:** Pet owner just got prescription, label is confusing

**Main points to cover:**
1. Dosage instructions - "Give 1 tablet by mouth every 12 hours" decoded
2. Active ingredient vs. brand name - Why both matter
3. Expiration dates and storage - Proper storage extends effectiveness
4. Warning labels - What "For animal use only" and other warnings mean
5. When to call vet - Missed doses, side effects, confusion

**Visual B-roll needs:**
- Close-ups of actual medication bottles and labels
- Pet owner reading label
- Calendar/clock for dosing schedule
- Refrigerator vs. cabinet storage
- Pet receiving medication

**Why this is video #1:**
- You said it's most interesting to you (motivation matters)
- Universal topic (all pet owners deal with medications)
- Clear value proposition
- Good sources readily available (VCA, AVMA, FDA)

**Expected performance:** Solid baseline. Should get 500-1,500 views in first week.

## Video 2: What Your Dog's Blood Panel Results Actually Mean

**Target length:** 7-8 minutes
**Keyword:** "dog blood test results," "understanding blood panel"
**Search intent:** Dog had blood work done, owner got results with confusing terms

**Main points to cover:**
1. CBC (Complete Blood Count) - Red cells, white cells, platelets explained
2. Chemistry panel - Liver enzymes, kidney function, glucose
3. What "normal range" means - Why your dog might be outside it and be fine
4. Red flags vs. monitor situations - When to worry vs. when to track
5. Questions to ask your vet about results

**Visual B-roll needs:**
- Actual blood test result printout (example/mock)
- Vet examining dog
- Blood draw process (if available)
- Charts/graphs showing normal ranges
- Dog playing (healthy dog imagery)

**Why this topic:**
- High emotional stakes (health anxiety)
- Very confusing terminology for pet owners
- Strong search volume
- Positions channel as translator of vet communications

**Expected performance:** Could be a hit if thumbnail/title nail the anxiety ("Is your dog's blood test ACTUALLY normal?")

## Video 3: When Dog Limping Is an Emergency vs. Wait-and-See

**Target length:** 6-7 minutes
**Keyword:** "dog limping emergency," "when to take limping dog to vet"
**Search intent:** Dog is limping, owner doesn't know if they should rush to vet or wait

**Main points to cover:**
1. Go to vet NOW signs - Won't bear weight, swelling, crying in pain, lethargy
2. Monitor for 24-48 hours signs - Mild limp, still playful, gradual onset
3. How to examine at home - Gently check paw, leg, range of motion
4. When to call vet for phone guidance - Unsure situations
5. Prevention tips - Nail trimming, avoiding high-impact on puppies

**Visual B-roll needs:**
- Dog limping (various severities)
- Owner examining dog's paw/leg
- Vet examining limping dog
- Dog playing normally
- Emergency vet clinic exterior

**Why this topic:**
- Decision-making content (high value)
- Timely (pet owner is in the moment, searching NOW)
- Could go viral if it helps someone make the right call
- Demonstrates practical utility of channel

**Expected performance:** Good CTR if thumbnail shows limping dog + question mark. Could be a hit.

## Video 10: Post-Surgery Care - What "Restrict Activity" Actually Means

**Target length:** 6-7 minutes
**Keyword:** "post surgery care for dogs," "restrict activity after surgery"

3. E-collar/cone management - Why it's necessary, how to help pet adjust
4. Incision care - What's normal, what's a red flag

**Visual B-roll needs:**
- Dog wearing e-collar/cone

- Healing incision (example, not graphic)

**Why this topic:**
- High-stakes (poor post-op care = complications)
- Very specific instructions owners struggle with
- Timely (owners searching RIGHT NOW after surgery)
- Practical, actionable advice

**Expected performance:** Strong performance from high-intent searchers. Good retention (they need this info).

- Controversial (lots of opinions = comments)

**Expected performance:** Moderate-high views, very high engagement (comments debating insurance value).

**Use this data to refine Month 2 calendar.**

## Flexibility Notes

**If a topic isn't working during research:**
- Can't find good sources → Swap for different topic
- Too complex to simplify → Replace with clearer topic
- Takes too long to research → Save for later, do simpler topic

**If production is taking too long:**
- Consider shorter videos (5 min instead of 7)
- Focus on simpler topics first
- Skip topics that require complex visuals

**If certain topics perform way better:**
- Double down on that category
- Create follow-up videos quickly
- Adjust Month 2 calendar to lean into what works
